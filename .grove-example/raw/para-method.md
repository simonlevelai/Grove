# The PARA Method

PARA is an organisational framework created by Tiago Forte as part of his Building a Second Brain methodology. The acronym stands for Projects, Areas, Resources, and Archives — four categories designed to capture every type of information a knowledge worker encounters.

## The Four Categories

**Projects** are short-term efforts with a defined goal and deadline. Examples: writing a report, planning an event, launching a feature. Projects are active and time-bound.

**Areas** are ongoing responsibilities without an end date. Examples: health, finances, professional development, team management. Areas require continuous attention but have no completion state.

**Resources** are topics of interest that may be useful in the future. Examples: graphic design, productivity techniques, market research. Resources are collected for reference rather than immediate action.

**Archives** are inactive items from the other three categories. Completed projects, areas you are no longer responsible for, and resources that have lost relevance all move here.

## Design Principles

PARA is built on the principle that information should be organised by actionability, not by topic. A note about "machine learning" might live in Projects (if you are building an ML feature), Areas (if you manage an ML team), or Resources (if you are simply interested in the topic). Context determines placement.

The system is deliberately tool-agnostic. The same four-folder structure works in a file system, a note-taking app, a cloud storage service, or an email client. Forte argues that consistency across tools reduces cognitive overhead.

## Maintenance

PARA requires periodic reviews to keep items in the correct category. A completed project moves to Archives. A resource that becomes relevant to an active project moves to Projects. This movement keeps the system alive and prevents it from becoming a graveyard of stale information.
